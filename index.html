Your codec was built for humans. Your consumer is a neural network.

H.264 and H.265 optimize for pixel fidelity — PSNR, smooth gradients, artifact-free motion. They are extraordinary engineering achievements designed for human viewers.

But surveillance video is no longer watched by humans. It is consumed by object detectors, trackers, and behavior analytics — systems that need semantic structure, not pixel perfection.

The mismatch is measurable. At 1.95 BPP, a detector on H.265 achieves 79.3% accuracy. The same detector on LeWM-VC at the same bitrate achieves 86.5%. Seven percentage points lost to pixels no human will ever see.

LeWM-VC — The product

How it works

LeWM-VC represents each video frame as a compact semantic latent — preserving what detectors need (object location, class, trajectory) while discarding texture and color detail that only human eyes care about.

  • ViT-Tiny encoder — 192 channels × 16 × 16 spatial grid per frame
  • JEPA temporal predictor — 8-layer transformer, forecasts next latent from 4 previous frames. No motion vectors.
  • GMM entropy model — 2-component mixture, 5.6× better than Laplace baseline
  • Residual coding — only the prediction error is transmitted

LeWM-Eval — Verification tool, ships with the codec

LeWM-Eval is a codec-agnostic evaluation framework that lets customers independently verify our numbers and compare LeWM-VC against any alternative. It ships with every deployment.

Bitrate matching

±5% BPP tolerance ensures comparisons are fair. Sweep CRF or λ to find the equivalent operating point.

Semantic probing

Identical 3-layer CNN probe on both LeWM-VC and baseline. Trained against frozen YOLOv5 teacher. Measures what matters.

Cross-teacher calibration

Multiple detectors (YOLOv5s, YOLOv5su) confirm rankings are not teacher-specific. Stable across teachers.
